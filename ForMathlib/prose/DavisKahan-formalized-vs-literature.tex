\begin{abstract}
This note states, in ordinary mathematical language, the Davis--Kahan
$\sin\Theta$ theorem that has been formalized in Lean, and pins down exactly how
it sits relative to the two cited source papers.  Briefly: what is formalized is
the \emph{Frobenius} $\sin\Theta$ bound
$\norm{\sinT}_{\F}\le\norm{S-T}_{\F}/\gap$, proved by the elementary cross-term /
Rayleigh-quotient mechanism of Davis, using a \emph{hybrid} eigengap between the
leading eigenvalues of one operator and the trailing eigenvalues of the other.
This is the deterministic core of the Yu--Wang--Samworth variant; the passage to
a population-only gap (their theorem) is a separate Weyl step, also formalized.
No resolvents or contour integrals are used.
\end{abstract}

\section{Setup and notation}

Let $\HH$ be a finite-dimensional inner product space over
$\mathbb K\in\{\mathbb R,\mathbb C\}$, $\dim\HH=n$, and let $T,S:\HH\to\HH$ be
self-adjoint.  Write the eigenvalues and an orthonormal eigenbasis of $T$ as
$\lambda_1(T),\dots,\lambda_n(T)$ and $u_1,\dots,u_n$, and likewise
$\lambda_1(S),\dots,\lambda_n(S)$ and $v_1,\dots,v_n$ for $S$.  Fix a block size
$d$; in the intended application the indices $1,\dots,d$ label the top-$d$
eigenvalues, but the statements below only ever use the index split
$\{1,\dots,d\}$ versus $\{d+1,\dots,n\}$, so no sorting is assumed a priori.

Let
\[
  P=\text{orthogonal projection onto }\spann(u_1,\dots,u_d),
  \qquad
  \widehat P=\text{orthogonal projection onto }\spann(v_1,\dots,v_d)
\]
be the projections onto the two leading spectral subspaces, and let
$\Theta=\Theta\bigl(\ran P,\ran\widehat P\bigr)$ be the vector of principal
angles between them.  The two norms used are the operator norm
$\norm{\cdot}_{\op}$ and the Frobenius (Hilbert--Schmidt) norm
$\norm{B}_{\F}^2=\tr(B^*B)=\sum_k\norm{Be_k}^2$ (any orthonormal basis $e_k$).

The separation hypothesis is a \emph{hybrid gap}: a number $\gap>0$ with
\begin{equation}\label{eq:gap}
  \gap\le\bigl|\lambda_i(T)-\lambda_j(S)\bigr|
  \qquad\text{for all }i\le d<j.
\end{equation}
It compares the leading eigenvalues of $T$ with the trailing eigenvalues of $S$
(one operator's block against the other's complement), which is the separation
that Davis's argument actually consumes.

\section{The formalized theorem}

Two equivalent presentations are formalized: an \emph{overlap} form written with
eigenvector inner products, and a \emph{projector} form written with the
basis-independent spectral projections.  They are the same statement, since the
overlap sum equals $\norm{\sinT}_{\F}^2$.

\begin{theorem}[Sharp Frobenius $\sin\Theta$; overlap form]\label{thm:sharp-overlap}
Let $T,S$ be self-adjoint and let $\gap>0$ satisfy \eqref{eq:gap}.  Then
\[
  \sum_{i\le d}\ \sum_{j>d}\ \bigl|\inner{u_i}{v_j}\bigr|^2
  \ \le\ \frac{\norm{S-T}_{\F}^2}{\gap^2}.
\]
Equivalently $\norm{\sinT}_{\F}\le\norm{S-T}_{\F}/\gap$.  There is no
operator-norm hypothesis and no dimension factor.
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[Sharp Frobenius $\sin\Theta$; projector form]\label{thm:sharp-proj}
Under the same hypotheses,
\[
  \norm{\widehat P-P}_{\F}^2\ \le\ \frac{2\,\norm{S-T}_{\F}^2}{\gap^2},
\]
i.e.\ $\norm{\widehat P-P}_{\F}\le\sqrt2\,\norm{S-T}_{\F}/\gap$.
\end{theorem}

The factor $2$ between the two forms is exact:
$\norm{\widehat P-P}_{\F}^2=2\norm{\sinT}_{\F}^2$, because
$\widehat P-P=\widehat P(I-P)-(I-\widehat P)P$ splits into two pieces with
orthogonal ranges of equal Frobenius norm (``how much each leading subspace leaks
out of the other''); each piece equals the overlap sum.

\paragraph{Crude operator-norm corollary.}
If one only controls the perturbation in operator norm, $\norm{S-T}_{\op}\le\eps$,
then $\norm{S-T}_{\F}^2\le n\,\eps^2$, and Theorems~\ref{thm:sharp-overlap}
and~\ref{thm:sharp-proj} degrade to
\[
  \sum_{i\le d}\sum_{j>d}\bigl|\inner{u_i}{v_j}\bigr|^2\le\frac{n\,\eps^2}{\gap^2},
  \qquad
  \norm{\widehat P-P}_{\F}^2\le\frac{2n\,\eps^2}{\gap^2}.
\]
The dimension factor $n$ enters at exactly this one step and nowhere else; it is
the price of converting an operator-norm bound into a Frobenius conclusion, and
it is tight for that conversion (take $S-T=\eps I$).

\paragraph{Rank / eigenvalue-floor corollary (the statistical setup).}
If $T$ is positive semidefinite of rank $d$ with its nonzero eigenvalues bounded
below by $\alpha$, and $S$ is $\eps$-close in operator norm with
$\eps\le\alpha/2$, then Weyl's inequality forces every trailing eigenvalue of $S$
below $\alpha/2$, so \eqref{eq:gap} holds with $\gap=\alpha/2$, and
\[
  \sum_{i\le d}\sum_{j>d}\bigl|\inner{u_i}{v_j}\bigr|^2\le\frac{4n\,\eps^2}{\alpha^2}.
\]

\section{The proof mechanism (one paragraph)}

For an eigenvector $v_j$ of $S$ with eigenvalue $\lambda_j(S)$ and an eigenvector
$u_i$ of $T$ with eigenvalue $\lambda_i(T)$, self-adjointness gives the
\emph{cross-term identity}
\begin{equation}\label{eq:cross}
  \inner{u_i}{(S-T)v_j}=\bigl(\lambda_j(S)-\lambda_i(T)\bigr)\inner{u_i}{v_j}.
\end{equation}
On a cross pair ($i\le d<j$), \eqref{eq:gap} and \eqref{eq:cross} give
$\gap^2\,|\inner{u_i}{v_j}|^2\le|\inner{u_i}{(S-T)v_j}|^2$.  Summing over the
cross block, and then enlarging to the full double sum (all terms nonnegative),
\[
  \gap^2\sum_{i\le d}\sum_{j>d}|\inner{u_i}{v_j}|^2
  \le\sum_{i,j}|\inner{u_i}{(S-T)v_j}|^2
  =\sum_j\norm{(S-T)v_j}^2
  =\norm{S-T}_{\F}^2,
\]
the middle equality being Parseval in the orthonormal basis $\{u_i\}$.  That is
the whole proof: no resolvents, no contour integrals.  The dimension factor
appears only if one replaces the last quantity by $n\eps^2$.

\section{Relation to Davis (1963/1970)}

The mechanism above is Davis's.  In ``The Rotation of Eigenvectors by a
Perturbation'' (\emph{J.\ Math.\ Anal.\ Appl.}\ \textbf{6}, 1963) the
total-rotation estimate for simple spectra reads, in Davis's notation
($A\rightsquigarrow T$, $A+H\rightsquigarrow S$, $\mathcal C^{\perp}U$ the
off-diagonal part of the matching unitary),
\[
  (\gamma')^2\,\norm{\mathcal C^{\perp}U}_{\F}^2
  \le\norm{H}_{\F}^2-\sum_i\bigl(\lambda_i-\lambda_i'\bigr)^2,
  \qquad
  \gamma_i=\min_{j\ne i}|\lambda_i-\lambda_j'|,
\]
proved by comparing $\inner{(A+H-\lambda_i')^2x_i}{x_i}$ from above and below --
exactly the identity \eqref{eq:cross}.  Our results are the same argument,
specialized to the two-block (leading/trailing) split rather than a full
eigenvector matching.  Of Davis's two refinements, one transfers and one does
not.  The \emph{off-diagonal} refinement does transfer, and is formalized: the
engine \texttt{\dots\_offDiag} bounds the overlap by the cross-block
$\norm{P(S-T)Q}_{\F}^2$ (and the one-sided residual \texttt{\dots\_residual} by
$\norm{(S-T)P}_{\F}^2$), the two-block analogues of controlling the rotation by
the off-diagonal part $\norm{\mathcal C^{\perp}H}_{\F}^2$ rather than the full
norm; Theorem~\ref{thm:sharp-overlap} then further enlarges this to
$\norm{S-T}_{\F}^2$.  The \emph{eigenvalue-displacement} refinement (subtracting
$\sum_i(\lambda_i-\lambda_i')^2$, equivalently the $(\gamma')^2$ correction to the
gap) does \emph{not} transfer: it is a feature of Davis's per-eigenvector matching,
whereas our block quantity $\norm{\sinT(\mathcal U,\mathcal V)}_{\F}^2$ discards the
within-block/diagonal part entirely, so there is nothing left to subtract.  Our
hybrid gap \eqref{eq:gap} is Davis's $\gamma_i$ restricted to the cross block.
The frequently cited companion is Davis \& Kahan, ``\dots\ III'', \emph{SIAM
J.\ Numer.\ Anal.}\ \textbf{7} (1970), which gives the $\sin\Theta$ theorem for
every unitarily invariant norm; the Frobenius norm is the instance formalized
here.

\section{Relation to Yu--Wang--Samworth (2015)}

Yu, Wang and Samworth (\emph{Biometrika} \textbf{102}, 2015) state, for symmetric
$\Sigma,\widehat\Sigma$ with population eigengap
$\Delta=\min(\lambda_{r-1}-\lambda_r,\ \lambda_s-\lambda_{s+1})$ built from
$\Sigma$ alone,
\[
  \norm{\sinT(\widehat V,V)}_{\F}
  \le\frac{2\min\{d^{1/2}\norm{\widehat\Sigma-\Sigma}_{\op},
                   \norm{\widehat\Sigma-\Sigma}_{\F}\}}{\Delta}.
\]
Their proof is the residual sandwich
$\Delta\norm{\sinT}_{\F}\le\norm{R}_{\F}\le
2\min\{d^{1/2}\norm{\cdot}_{\op},\norm{\cdot}_{\F}\}$ with
$R=\widehat V\Lambda-\Sigma\widehat V$; the left inequality is a deterministic
separation estimate, the right one a perturbation estimate (Weyl and
Hoffman--Wielandt), and the factor $2$ is the cost of moving from a mixed
population/sample separation to the population-only gap $\Delta$.

Our development is the two pieces YWS fuse, kept apart:
\begin{itemize}
\item Theorem~\ref{thm:sharp-overlap} (with the \emph{hybrid} gap \eqref{eq:gap})
  is the deterministic separation estimate --- YWS's left inequality --- but with
  constant $1$ rather than $2$, because the hybrid gap already accounts for where
  the trailing eigenvalues sit, so no Hoffman--Wielandt eigenvalue-displacement
  term is incurred.  This is precisely the ``classical Davis--Kahan with a mixed
  population/sample separation'' that YWS's remark describes as the usual
  workaround.
\item \texttt{gap\_of\_eigengap} is the Weyl step that turns a population-only
  spectral gap of $T$ (leading eigenvalues $\ge a$, trailing $\le b$) into the
  hybrid gap $(a-b)-\eps$ --- the move YWS package into their theorem's denominator
  (their $\Delta=\lambda_d-\lambda_{d+1}$ is $a-b$).  \texttt{gap\_of\_rank\_floor}
  is the special case $a=\alpha$, $b=0$.
\end{itemize}
So the Frobenius conclusion is the same; the difference is bookkeeping about the
gap, and YWS's $d^{1/2}$ operator-norm branch is also available
(\texttt{\dots\_opNorm}, $\mathrm{overlap}\le d\,\eps^2/\gap^2$, obtained from the
one-sided residual by bounding its $\le d$ leading columns).  One remaining gap
in coverage: we formalize only the symmetric $\sin\Theta$ statement, not YWS's
aligned-basis (orthogonal Procrustes) corollary or their singular-vector
extension.
